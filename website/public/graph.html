Meta clusters

Looking at the data, we need to remove complexity from the network. Some of the keywords are co-occurring with almost every keyword. We call them "Meta Clusters". Meta clusters describe PLM in a very generic matter and only add trivial information but a lot of noise to the statistics. Thus you might want to remove them from the graph.

Meta clusters can be identified by two criteria:

  • A very high degree (most authors tend to use them to describe PLM)
  • Very constant over time (they have been continuously used to describe PLM)

Trend

Another interesting measure is the trend. Do the authors tend to use a certain keyword more often or did they stop to use it? We consider a good measure of the trend is the linear regression over the histogram of a keyword. The histogram plots the number of papers using a certain keyword per year (occurrences per year).
